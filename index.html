About me

I'm a PhD Student of Meteorology and Physical Oceanography under the supervision of Dr. Gustavo Goni (AOML/NOAA) and Dr. Ben Kirtman at Rosenstiel School of Marine and Atmospheric Sciences (RSMAS), Miami, US. My research interests include, but are not limited to: How coastal sea level trends are influenced by Western Boundary Currents (WBC) and local currents; WBC dynamics and variability.

Education

I got my BSc. degree in oceanography from University of Bahia (UFBA), Brazil and MSc. degree in meteorology from University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ), Brazil. During that time, I joined Dr. Mauro Cirano's research group, the Physical Oceanography Laboratory, where we conducted studies about the Brazil Current dynamics and how different datasets capture its variability. If you want to know more about my academic background, publications, and skills download my CV, my BSc/MSc theses, or my Lattes CV (for Brazilians) using the links bellow.
